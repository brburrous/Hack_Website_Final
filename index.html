Brian

After a refreshing 5 hours of sleep filled with nightmares of over constrained solidworks assemblies and under defined sketches, it was time to start day 2.

I left the assembly alone and started designing a system to release a ball from the robot. It was clear from the kit of parts that the creators of this challenge had a solution in mind. Ignoring this, I strew all my parts over the kitchen table and did my best impression of a child playing with legos.

I took various parts and stuck them together to test different potential mechanisms. Most were pretty bad.

Finally, I stumbled upon a solution. Placing two angle brackets at a 90 degree angle from each other, I could create a corner that a round object could rest in. This assembly was placed next to a servo with an arm attached. In the closed position, the arm would sit in front of the round object. All it takes to release the ball is to rotate the servo arm by a few degrees, causing the ball to roll out consistently.

Don‚Äôt let the simplicity fool you. This design proved to work very consistently.

After that, I starting building the full robot. The only other difficulty I ran into was mounting the breadboard. The board was supposed to be mounted to a wooden platform sized to fit the robot frame. However, our ball mechanism and servo placement got in the way, so I had to rotate the board 90 degrees and attach it with screws, leaving half the board hanging off the edge of the bot.



Also‚Ä¶I failed to mention, this was after two failed attempts at attaching the board. The first attempt, I stuck the breadboard directly to the robot frame, but forgot to account for the servo arm position. I had to rip the board off, which actually destroyed the board.
